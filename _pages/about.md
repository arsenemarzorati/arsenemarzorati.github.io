Welcome on my personal website! 

I completed my PhD at [Inria Lyon](https://www.inria.fr/fr/centre-inria-de-lyon) in December 2025 under the supervision of [Samuel Bernard](https://math.univ-lyon1.fr/~bernard/address.html) and [Jonathan Rouzaud-Cornabas](https://perso.liris.cnrs.fr/jrouzaud/). I was a member of the Inria reserach teams [BioTic](https://team.inria.fr/biotic/en/) and [MUSICS](https://team.inria.fr/musics/en/).

My research lies at the intersection of numerical analysis, high-performance computing, and mathematical modelling. My doctoral work focused on the developmeent and analysis of mixed-precision numercial solvers for large-scale systems of ordinary differential equations (ODEs), with applications to computational biology. This research combined theoretical analysis (stability, error control) with practical implementation challenges related to performance and efficient use of computing architecture. More broadly, I am interested in the design of robust, scalable, and structure-aware numerical methods for complex dynamical systems arising in applied sciences.

I am currently seeking a postdoctoral position, with a particular interest in climate-related modelling and large-scale environmental simulations.
